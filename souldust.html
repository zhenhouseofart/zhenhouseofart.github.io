CLIENT

Souldust

Souldust is a Malaysian streetwear brand that aims to help the young express their inner emotions as mental health grows more prevalent in the aging world. Fashion has always been used as a mode of self-expression even to as far as to create an individual and separate identity from their elders. (Bovone, 2006) It is representation of one’s perception of themselves and Souldust acts to be a part of that expression. The brand prioritises comfort with the Malaysian weather appropriate material and high quality prints.

The experimental project was launched to gauge the market for the meaningful design for streetwear in Malaysia as Souldust is the resulting product of multiple case studies and a thesis paper. The brand utilises a style that is fun and descriptive of mental health in a witty way, with its colourful, humorous and almost ironic depictions of mental health issues.

The project started out as a research project studying the unique characteristics of urban streetwear (and its relevance to the Malaysian market) whereby multiple case studies, surveys and other modes of research were conducted to support the study. We found that there was a prominent market for the street fashion industry in Malaysia and after countless fabric surveys, print sourcing, working out logistical technicalities, market strategising and brand building, Souldust was launched. Although our team was small, it was a successful launch with all products selling out within the month of our launch.

Reference. Bovone, L. (2006). Urban style cultures and urban cultural production in Milan: Postmodern identity and the transformation of fashion. Poetics (Amsterdam), 34(6), 370-382. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.poetic.2006.10.004
